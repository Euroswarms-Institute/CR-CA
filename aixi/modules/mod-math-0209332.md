# Module hook: math/0209332 (hypercomputation survey)

**URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/math/0209332

**Bibliographic note:** Toby Ord, *Hypercomputation: computing more than the Turing machine* (arXiv:math/0209332v1, math.LO, Sep 2002). A **survey** of models grouped under “hypercomputation” (alleged power strictly beyond Turing machines), how they relate to classical computability theory, comparisons of strength, physical realizability, and philosophical consequences (e.g. framing of Gödel and Chaitin-style results). **Not** a sequential-decision, RL, or constructive AIXI / mixture-learning paper.

## Role in AIXI stack

- **Families A / B / C:** **None as an algorithmic ingredient.** Ord does not define percept kernels, \(\xi\) update rules, planners, or return mixtures.
- **Where it belongs relative to `IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` §3 (\(\xi\)):** §3 specifies a **Bayesian mixture** over a **finite, tractable** model class \(\mathcal{M}\) (CTW / neural ensembles, etc.). A **literal** universal Solomonoff-style mixture over “all computable” environments sits at the edge of classical computability (halting-related constants, semimeasure normalization). Ord’s survey is the **computability-boundary** reading list for *why* productized \(\xi\) must stay **Turing-bounded** and **finitely indexed**: anything marketed as “full universality” without finite \(\mathcal{M}\) drifts toward the hypercomputation / Church–Turing debate space, not implementable APIs.
